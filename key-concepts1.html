How to approach a question:

  1. “Guess” \( \varphi(x, y) \) for a structure.
    • This procedure is often iterative. In this module, you will be provided with a stress function.
  2. Apply biharmonic relationship
    • The biharmonic equation is: \[ \nabla^4 \varphi = \frac{\partial^4 \varphi}{\partial x^4} + 2\frac{\partial^4 \varphi}{\partial x^2 \partial y^2} + \frac{\partial^4 \varphi}{\partial y^4} = 0 \]
  3. Calculate stresses by differentiation of \( \varphi(x, y) \)
    • Stresses are derived as: \[ \sigma_x = \frac{\partial^2 \varphi}{\partial x^2}, \quad \sigma_y = \frac{\partial^2 \varphi}{\partial y^2}, \quad \tau_{xy} = -\frac{\partial^2 \varphi}{\partial x \partial y} \]
  4. Compare stresses from 3) to the boundary conditions you see visually in the structure.
    • This step involves ensuring the calculated stresses match the boundary conditions.
  5. Calculate strains from constitutive law
    • Strains are calculated using: \[ \epsilon_x = \frac{\sigma_x}{E} - \nu \left( \frac{\sigma_y}{E} \right), \quad \epsilon_y = -\nu \left( \frac{\sigma_x}{E} \right) + \frac{\sigma_y}{E}, \quad \gamma_{xy} = \frac{1}{G} \tau_{xy} \]
  6. Calculate displacement field by integration
    • Displacements are obtained by integrating strain components: \[ u(x, y) = \int \epsilon_x \, dx \]

These steps can be utilised to answer any question in Topic 1!
