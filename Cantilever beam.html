Cantilever Beam Analysis

1. Basic Situation of Cantilever Beam

The boundary conditions of the cantilever beam are given as follows:

Left end (fixed end): \[ u = 0 \quad v = 0 \quad \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} - \beta \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = 0 \]
Right end (free end): \[ N = 0 \quad Q = -F_0 \quad M = -M_0 \]

Beam Parameters

Poisson's ratio: \[ \nu = 0.3 \] Elastic modulus: \[ E = 210 \times 10^9 \, \text{Pa} \] Moment of inertia: \[ I = 1 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^4 \] Distributed load: \[ q = 1000 \, \text{N/m} \] Beam length: \[ l = 2 \, \text{m} \] Beam height: \[ h = 0.1 \, \text{m} \]

2. Parameter β Determination

\[ \beta = \frac{8 + 9\nu}{2 + \nu} \]

where \(\nu\) is Poisson's ratio.
