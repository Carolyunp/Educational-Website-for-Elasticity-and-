Stress Function Analysis of a Cantilever Beam

Problem Statement

A cantilever beam of length \( L \) and depth \( 2h \) is in a state of plane strain. The cantilever is rigidly supported at \( x = L \) and is loaded as shown in Figure 1. Examine the following stress function in relation to this problem:

\[ \phi = \frac{p}{20h^3} \left( 15h^2 x^2 y - 5x^2 y^3 - 2h^2 y^3 + y^5 \right), \]

and show that the stresses acting on the boundary are as required, except for a distributed direct stress on the end of the beam which exerts no resultant force or moment.

Questions

  1. Derive the stress components from the given stress function. [8 marks]
  2. Show that the boundary conditions are satisfied for the given loading conditions. [3 marks]
  3. Confirm the stress distribution and its agreement with beam theory. [4 marks]

Solution Process

1. Stress Components

Given the functional form for the stress function provided in the question:

\[ \phi = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( 15h^2 x^2 y - 5x^2 y^3 - 2h^2 y^3 + y^5 \right) \]

The stress components can be derived from the given stress function:

\[ \sigma_x = \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial y^2} = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( -30x^2 y - 12h^2 y + 20y^3 \right) \]

\[ \sigma_y = \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial x^2} = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( 30h^2 y - 10y^3 \right) \]

\[ \tau_{xy} = -\frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( -30h^2 x + 30xy^2 \right) \]

2. Verification of Boundary Conditions

At \( y = h \), we have:

\[ \sigma_y = P, \quad \tau_{xy} = 0 \]

At \( y = -h \), we have:

\[ \sigma_y = -P, \quad \tau_{xy} = 0 \]

which satisfy the expected loading conditions.

At \( x = 0 \), a distributed direct stress exists:

\[ \sigma_x |_{x=0} = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( -12h^2 y + 20y^3 \right) \]

3. Resultant Force and Moment

The resultant force is obtained by integrating over the depth:

\[ \int_{-h}^{h} \sigma_x \, dy = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left[ -6h y^2 + 5y^4 \right]_{-h}^{h} = 0 \]

The moment is given by:

\[ \int_{-h}^{h} \sigma_x y \, dy = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left[ -4h^2 y + 4y^5 \right]_{-h}^{h} = 0 \]

4. Agreement with Beam Theory

For large \( x \) values, where \( x \gg h \), we observe:

\[ \sigma_x \to -\frac{3P x^2 y}{2h^3} \]

which is verified by beam theory using:

\[ M = -\frac{2P x^2}{2}, \quad I = \frac{(2h)^3}{12} \]

so that:

\[ \sigma_x = \frac{M y}{I} = -\frac{3P x^2 y}{2h^3} \]

which confirms that the derived stress function is consistent with classical beam bending theory.

Problem Statement

The cantilever shown in Figure 2 is of narrow rectangular section of unit width and is subjected to a couple \( M \) at the end \( x = 0 \). Suggest a suitable stress function and determine the displacements \( u \) and \( v \) in the \( x \)-\( y \) plane.

Questions

  1. Define the boundary conditions and derive a suitable stress function for the given cantilever beam. [8 marks]
  2. Determine the stress components and verify their consistency with the boundary conditions. [3 marks]
  3. Compute the displacements \( u \) and \( v \) in the \( x \)-\( y \) plane. [4 marks]

Solution Process

1. Boundary Conditions and Stress Function

To satisfy the loading and boundary conditions:

- There must be no shear component or loading on the longitudinal boundaries \( y = \pm c \).

- The bending moment at the end \( x = 0 \) must be nonzero, but the net force components must be zero.

From equilibrium conditions:

\[ \int_{-c}^{c} y \sigma_x \, dy = M \]

\[ \int_{-c}^{c} \sigma_x |_{x=0} \, dy = 0, \quad \int_{-c}^{c} \tau_{xy} |_{x=0} \, dy = 0 \]

We assume the stress function:

\[ \phi = \frac{F}{6} y^3 \]

2. Stress Components

The stress components are derived from the stress function:

\[ \sigma_x = \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial y^2} = Fy \]

\[ \sigma_y = \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial x^2} = 0, \quad \tau_{xy} = -\frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial x \partial y} = 0 \]

Since \( \sigma_y = 0 \) and \( \tau_{xy} = 0 \), the boundary conditions on \( y = \pm c \) are automatically satisfied.

To satisfy the moment equilibrium:

\[ \int_{-c}^{c} y \sigma_x \, dy = -M \]

which gives:

\[ F = -\frac{3M}{2c^3} \]

Thus, the stress function becomes:

\[ \phi = -\frac{3M}{6} \frac{y^3}{2c^3} \]

and the final stress components are:

\[ \sigma_x = -\frac{3M}{2c^3} y, \quad \sigma_y = \tau_{xy} = 0. \]

3. Displacement Calculations

Using Hooke's law:

\[ \epsilon_x = \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\sigma_x}{E} = -\frac{3M}{2c^3 E} y \]

\[ \epsilon_y = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} = -\frac{\nu}{E} \sigma_x = \frac{\nu 3M}{2c^3 E} y \]

Integrating gives:

\[ u = -\frac{3M}{2c^3 E} yx + f(y), \quad v = \frac{\nu 3M}{4E c^3} y^2 + g(x) \]

4. Solving for Unknown Functions

The shear strain condition gives:

\[ 0 = -\frac{3M}{2c^3 E} x + \frac{df}{dy} + \frac{dg}{dx} \]

Since terms involving \( x \) and \( y \) must be equal separately:

\[ \frac{df}{dy} = r, \quad \frac{dg}{dx} -\frac{3M}{2c^3 E} x = s \]

where \( r \) and \( s \) are constants, and we require \( r = -s \).

Integrating:

\[ f = -sy + f_0, \quad g = \frac{3M}{2c^3 E} \frac{x^2}{2} + sx + g_0 \]

Thus, the displacement equations become:

\[ u = -\frac{3M}{2c^3 E} yx - sy + f_0 \]

\[ v = \frac{\nu 3M}{4c^3 E} y^2 + \frac{3M}{2c^3 E} \frac{x^2}{2} + sx + g_0 \]

5. Applying Boundary Conditions

At \( x = L, y = 0 \), we require:

\[ u = v = \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} = 0. \]

Solving for constants:

\[ f_0 = 0, \quad s = -\frac{3M L}{2c^3 E}, \quad g_0 = \frac{3M L^2}{4c^3 E} \]

Thus, the final displacement equations are:

\[ u = -\frac{3M}{2c^3 E} yx + \frac{3M L}{2c^3 E} y \]

\[ v = \frac{\nu 3M}{4c^3 E} y^2 + \frac{3M}{2c^3 E} \frac{x^2}{2} - \frac{3M L}{2c^3 E} x + \frac{3M L^2}{4c^3 E} \]
