Stress Analysis Around a Circular Hole in a Plate

Problem Statement

A section of an aircraft wing skin can be analyzed by the configuration of Figure 6. The plate is subjected to uniform tensile stress of magnitude \( S \) in the \( x \) and \( y \) directions, and at the origin, it contains a hole with radius \( a \). Calculate the stress state around the hole, commenting on the magnitude of these stresses at the boundary of the hole.

Questions

  1. Derive the stress field equations around the hole based on the given stress function. [8 marks]
  2. Determine the boundary conditions and calculate the stress values at the hole's boundary. [3 marks]
  3. Identify the locations where the maximum and minimum stresses occur and provide their values. [4 marks]

Solution Process

1. Stress Function and Governing Equations

The solution of the biharmonic equation in cylindrical polar coordinates is obtained using separation of variables:

\[ \phi = R(r) \Theta(\theta) \]

One possible solution is: \[ \phi = \left( A r^2 + B r^4 + \frac{C}{r^2} + D \right) \cos 2\theta \]

The general solution for the stress function in a plate with a circular hole subjected to a uniform tensile stress \( S \) is: \[ \phi = \frac{S}{2} (2r^2 - a^2 \ln r) - \frac{S}{4} \left( r^2 + \frac{a^4}{r^2} - 2a^2 \right) \cos 2\theta \]

2. Stress Field Expressions

The stress components derived from the stress function are:

\[ \sigma_r = \frac{S}{2} \left( 1 - \frac{a^2}{r^2} \right) + \frac{S}{2} \left( 1 + \frac{3a^4}{r^4} - \frac{4a^2}{r^2} \right) \cos 2\theta \]

\[ \sigma_{\theta} = \frac{S}{2} \left( 1 + \frac{a^2}{r^2} \right) - \frac{S}{2} \left( 1 + \frac{3a^4}{r^4} \right) \cos 2\theta \]

\[ \tau_{r\theta} = -\frac{S}{2} \left( 1 - \frac{3a^4}{r^4} + \frac{2a^2}{r^2} \right) \sin 2\theta \]

3. Boundary Conditions and Stress at the Hole

At the boundary of the hole \( r = a \), the stress components simplify to:

\[ \sigma_r = 0, \quad \sigma_{\theta} = S - 2S \cos 2\theta, \quad \tau_{r\theta} = 0 \]

4. Maximum and Minimum Stresses

The circumferential stress reaches its maximum at \( \theta = \frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{3\pi}{2} \):

\[ \sigma_{\theta, \max} = 3S \]

The minimum circumferential stress occurs at \( \theta = 0, \pi \):

\[ \sigma_{\theta, \min} = -S \]

5. Alternative Expression for Stresses

Another equivalent formulation for stress components is:

\[ \sigma_r = S \left( 1 - \frac{a^2}{r^2} \right) \]

\[ \sigma_{\theta} = S \left( 1 + \frac{a^2}{r^2} \right) \]

\[ \tau_{r\theta} = 0 \]

At the edge of the hole \( r = a \), we obtain:

\[ \sigma_r = \tau_{r\theta} = 0, \quad \sigma_{\theta} = 2S \]

Thus, the transverse tensile stress reduces the stress concentration at the hole.
