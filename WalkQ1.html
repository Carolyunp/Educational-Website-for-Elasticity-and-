Problem in Polar Coordinates

Problem Statement

A semicircular tunnel supporting vertical loads can be simplified, due to symmetry, to the beam setup of Figure 1. It is subjected to a load of magnitude \( P \) on surface \( CD \). For the solution of the stress, strain, and displacement fields of this beam, consider the general form of a stress function:

\[ \phi(r,\theta) = \left( S r^3 + \frac{N}{r} + O r + Q r \log r \right) \sin \theta \]

where \( S, N, O, Q \) are constants.

Questions

  1. Specify all boundary conditions on all four surfaces, connecting them with the appropriate stress field components. [8 marks]
  2. Write complete relationships for the stress field (i.e. \( \sigma_r(r, \theta) \), \( \sigma_{\theta}(r, \theta) \), \( \tau_{r\theta}(r, \theta) \)) in terms of the position \( (r, \theta) \), parameters \( S, N, O, Q \), and radii \( a, b \). [3 marks]
  3. Form a system of equations that can be used to express the unknown parameters \( S, N, O, Q \) in terms of the known quantities \( P, a, b \) (but do not solve this system). [4 marks]

Solution Process

1. Boundary Conditions

The boundary conditions on all four surfaces are as follows:

  • Surface AD: \( \sigma_r(a, \theta) = \tau_{r\theta}(a, \theta) = 0 \)
  • Surface BC: \( \sigma_r(b, \theta) = \tau_{r\theta}(b, \theta) = 0 \)
  • Surface CD: \[ \int_{a}^{b} \tau_{r\theta}(r,0) \, dr = P \] \[ F_\theta = \int_{a}^{b} \sigma_{\theta}(r,0) \, dr = 0 \] \[ M = \int_{a}^{b} \sigma_{\theta}(r,0) r \, dr = 0 \]
  • Surface AB: Using equilibrium of forces and moments: \[ \int_{a}^{b} \sigma_{\theta}(r, \frac{\pi}{2}) \, dr = -P \] \[ M = \int_{a}^{b} \sigma_{\theta}(r, \frac{\pi}{2}) r \, dr = P \frac{a+b}{2} \] \[ \int_{a}^{b} \tau_{r\theta}(r, \frac{\pi}{2}) \, dr = 0 \]

2. Stress Field Expressions

The stress components derived from the stress function are:

\[ \sigma_r = - \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial \theta^2} = \left( 2Ar - \frac{2B}{r^3} + \frac{D}{r} \right) \sin\theta \]

\[ \sigma_{\theta} = \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial r^2} = \left( 6Ar + \frac{2B}{r^3} + D \right) \sin\theta \]

\[ \tau_{r\theta} = - \frac{\partial}{\partial r} \left( \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial \theta} \right) = -\left( 2Ar - \frac{2B}{r^3} + D \right) \cos\theta \]

3. System of Equations for \( S, N, O, Q \)

From the boundary conditions, we obtain the following system of equations:

\[ 2Aa - \frac{2B}{a^3} + \frac{D}{a} = 0 \]

\[ 2Ab - \frac{2B}{b^3} + \frac{D}{b} = 0 \]

\[ A (b^2 - a^2) + B \left( \frac{1}{b^2} - \frac{1}{a^2} \right) + D \log \left(\frac{b}{a} \right) = -P \quad \]

\[ 3A (b^2 - a^2) + B \left( \frac{1}{b^2} - \frac{1}{a^2} \right) + D \log \left(\frac{b}{a} \right) = -P \quad \]

\[ 2A (b^3 - a^3) - 2B \left( \frac{1}{b} - \frac{1}{a} \right) + D (b - a) = P \frac{a + b}{2} \quad \]
