Problem in Polar Coordinates

Problem Statement

A semicircular tunnel supporting vertical loads can be simplified, due to symmetry, to the beam setup of Figure 1. It is subjected to a load of magnitude \( P \) on surface \( CD \). For the solution of the stress, strain, and displacement fields of this beam, consider the general form of a stress function:

\[ \phi(r,\theta) = \left( S r^3 + \frac{N}{r} + O r + Q r \log r \right) \sin \theta \]

where \( S, N, O, Q \) are constants.

Questions

  1. Specify all boundary conditions on all four surfaces, connecting them with the appropriate stress field components. [8 marks]
  2. Write complete relationships for the stress field (i.e. \( \sigma_r(r, \theta) \), \( \sigma_{\theta}(r, \theta) \), \( \tau_{r\theta}(r, \theta) \)) in terms of the position \( (r, \theta) \), parameters \( S, N, O, Q \), and radii \( a, b \). [3 marks]
  3. Form a system of equations that can be used to express the unknown parameters \( S, N, O, Q \) in terms of the known quantities \( P, a, b \) (but do not solve this system). [4 marks]

Solution Process

1. Boundary Conditions

The boundary conditions on all four surfaces are as follows:

  • Surface AD: \( \sigma_r(a, \theta) = \tau_{r\theta}(a, \theta) = 0 \)
  • Surface BC: \( \sigma_r(b, \theta) = \tau_{r\theta}(b, \theta) = 0 \)
  • Surface CD: \[ \int_{a}^{b} \tau_{r\theta}(r,0) \, dr = P \] \[ F_\theta = \int_{a}^{b} \sigma_{\theta}(r,0) \, dr = 0 \] \[ M = \int_{a}^{b} \sigma_{\theta}(r,0) r \, dr = 0 \]
  • Surface AB: Using equilibrium of forces and moments: \[ \int_{a}^{b} \sigma_{\theta}(r, \frac{\pi}{2}) \, dr = -P \] \[ M = \int_{a}^{b} \sigma_{\theta}(r, \frac{\pi}{2}) r \, dr = P \frac{a+b}{2} \] \[ \int_{a}^{b} \tau_{r\theta}(r, \frac{\pi}{2}) \, dr = 0 \]

2. Stress Field Expressions

The stress components derived from the stress function are:

\[ \sigma_r = - \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r^2} \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial \theta^2} = \left( 2Ar - \frac{2B}{r^3} + \frac{D}{r} \right) \sin\theta \]

\[ \sigma_{\theta} = \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial r^2} = \left( 6Ar + \frac{2B}{r^3} + D \right) \sin\theta \]

\[ \tau_{r\theta} = - \frac{\partial}{\partial r} \left( \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial \theta} \right) = -\left( 2Ar - \frac{2B}{r^3} + D \right) \cos\theta \]

3. System of Equations for \( S, N, O, Q \)

From the boundary conditions, we obtain the following system of equations:

\[ 2Aa - \frac{2B}{a^3} + \frac{D}{a} = 0 \]

\[ 2Ab - \frac{2B}{b^3} + \frac{D}{b} = 0 \]

\[ A (b^2 - a^2) + B \left( \frac{1}{b^2} - \frac{1}{a^2} \right) + D \log \left(\frac{b}{a} \right) = -P \quad \]

\[ 3A (b^2 - a^2) + B \left( \frac{1}{b^2} - \frac{1}{a^2} \right) + D \log \left(\frac{b}{a} \right) = -P \quad \]

\[ 2A (b^3 - a^3) - 2B \left( \frac{1}{b} - \frac{1}{a} \right) + D (b - a) = P \frac{a + b}{2} \quad \]

Stress Analysis Around a Circular Hole in a Plate

Problem Statement

A section of an aircraft wing skin can be analyzed by the configuration of Figure 6. The plate is subjected to uniform tensile stress of magnitude \( S \) in the \( x \) and \( y \) directions, and at the origin, it contains a hole with radius \( a \). Calculate the stress state around the hole, commenting on the magnitude of these stresses at the boundary of the hole.

Questions

  1. Derive the stress field equations around the hole based on the given stress function. [8 marks]
  2. Determine the boundary conditions and calculate the stress values at the hole's boundary. [3 marks]
  3. Identify the locations where the maximum and minimum stresses occur and provide their values. [4 marks]

Solution Process

1. Stress Function and Governing Equations

The solution of the biharmonic equation in cylindrical polar coordinates is obtained using separation of variables:

\[ \phi = R(r) \Theta(\theta) \]

One possible solution is: \[ \phi = \left( A r^2 + B r^4 + \frac{C}{r^2} + D \right) \cos 2\theta \]

The general solution for the stress function in a plate with a circular hole subjected to a uniform tensile stress \( S \) is: \[ \phi = \frac{S}{2} (2r^2 - a^2 \ln r) - \frac{S}{4} \left( r^2 + \frac{a^4}{r^2} - 2a^2 \right) \cos 2\theta \]

2. Stress Field Expressions

The stress components derived from the stress function are:

\[ \sigma_r = \frac{S}{2} \left( 1 - \frac{a^2}{r^2} \right) + \frac{S}{2} \left( 1 + \frac{3a^4}{r^4} - \frac{4a^2}{r^2} \right) \cos 2\theta \]

\[ \sigma_{\theta} = \frac{S}{2} \left( 1 + \frac{a^2}{r^2} \right) - \frac{S}{2} \left( 1 + \frac{3a^4}{r^4} \right) \cos 2\theta \]

\[ \tau_{r\theta} = -\frac{S}{2} \left( 1 - \frac{3a^4}{r^4} + \frac{2a^2}{r^2} \right) \sin 2\theta \]

3. Boundary Conditions and Stress at the Hole

At the boundary of the hole \( r = a \), the stress components simplify to:

\[ \sigma_r = 0, \quad \sigma_{\theta} = S - 2S \cos 2\theta, \quad \tau_{r\theta} = 0 \]

4. Maximum and Minimum Stresses

The circumferential stress reaches its maximum at \( \theta = \frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{3\pi}{2} \):

\[ \sigma_{\theta, \max} = 3S \]

The minimum circumferential stress occurs at \( \theta = 0, \pi \):

\[ \sigma_{\theta, \min} = -S \]

5. Alternative Expression for Stresses

Another equivalent formulation for stress components is:

\[ \sigma_r = S \left( 1 - \frac{a^2}{r^2} \right) \]

\[ \sigma_{\theta} = S \left( 1 + \frac{a^2}{r^2} \right) \]

\[ \tau_{r\theta} = 0 \]

At the edge of the hole \( r = a \), we obtain:

\[ \sigma_r = \tau_{r\theta} = 0, \quad \sigma_{\theta} = 2S \]

Thus, the transverse tensile stress reduces the stress concentration at the hole.

Stress Function Analysis of a Cantilever Beam

Problem Statement

A cantilever beam of length \( L \) and depth \( 2h \) is in a state of plane strain. The cantilever is rigidly supported at \( x = L \) and is loaded as shown in Figure 1. Examine the following stress function in relation to this problem:

\[ \phi = \frac{p}{20h^3} \left( 15h^2 x^2 y - 5x^2 y^3 - 2h^2 y^3 + y^5 \right), \]

and show that the stresses acting on the boundary are as required, except for a distributed direct stress on the end of the beam which exerts no resultant force or moment.

Questions

  1. Derive the stress components from the given stress function. [8 marks]
  2. Show that the boundary conditions are satisfied for the given loading conditions. [3 marks]
  3. Confirm the stress distribution and its agreement with beam theory. [4 marks]

Solution Process

1. Stress Components

Given the functional form for the stress function provided in the question:

\[ \phi = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( 15h^2 x^2 y - 5x^2 y^3 - 2h^2 y^3 + y^5 \right) \]

The stress components can be derived from the given stress function:

\[ \sigma_x = \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial y^2} = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( -30x^2 y - 12h^2 y + 20y^3 \right) \]

\[ \sigma_y = \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial x^2} = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( 30h^2 y - 10y^3 \right) \]

\[ \tau_{xy} = -\frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial x \partial y} = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( -30h^2 x + 30xy^2 \right) \]

2. Verification of Boundary Conditions

At \( y = h \), we have:

\[ \sigma_y = P, \quad \tau_{xy} = 0 \]

At \( y = -h \), we have:

\[ \sigma_y = -P, \quad \tau_{xy} = 0 \]

which satisfy the expected loading conditions.

At \( x = 0 \), a distributed direct stress exists:

\[ \sigma_x |_{x=0} = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left( -12h^2 y + 20y^3 \right) \]

3. Resultant Force and Moment

The resultant force is obtained by integrating over the depth:

\[ \int_{-h}^{h} \sigma_x \, dy = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left[ -6h y^2 + 5y^4 \right]_{-h}^{h} = 0 \]

The moment is given by:

\[ \int_{-h}^{h} \sigma_x y \, dy = \frac{P}{20h^3} \left[ -4h^2 y + 4y^5 \right]_{-h}^{h} = 0 \]

4. Agreement with Beam Theory

For large \( x \) values, where \( x \gg h \), we observe:

\[ \sigma_x \to -\frac{3P x^2 y}{2h^3} \]

which is verified by beam theory using:

\[ M = -\frac{2P x^2}{2}, \quad I = \frac{(2h)^3}{12} \]

so that:

\[ \sigma_x = \frac{M y}{I} = -\frac{3P x^2 y}{2h^3} \]

which confirms that the derived stress function is consistent with classical beam bending theory.
